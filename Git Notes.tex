\abstract
{
%Not too long ago, it was found that certain 4d $\nn{1}$ Lagrangians experience supersymmetry enhancement at their IR fixed point, thereby giving a Lagrangian description for a plethora of Argyres-Douglas theories \cite{Maruyoshi:2016tqk,Maruyoshi:2016aim,Agarwal:2016pjo,Agarwal:2017roi,Benvenuti:2017bpg}.  A generic feature of these Lagrangians is that a number of gauge invariant operators decouple (as free fields) along the RG-flow. The authors of \cite{Benvenuti:2017lle, Benvenuti:2017kud} considered dimensional reduction of these Lagrangians to 3d spacetime. They argued that in order to match the dimensionally reduced Lagrangians with the known results for dimensional reduction of AD theories, one needs to add ``flipping fields'' to the Lagrangian. The purpose of these flipping fields is take into account the decoupling of operators by removing them from the chiral ring. We study this proposal further and find a counter example, such that the addition of a flipping field to the dimensionally reduced Lagrangian actually spoils the expected match. In the process we find two new dual Lagrangians for the so called 3d $T[SU(2)]$ theory.
